Welcome to the Evelyn Oldfield Unit

Enabling BAMER communities for nearly 25 years

Our Mission

The EOU aims to provide, develop and coordinate support services for marginalized and disadvantaged individuals and their communities - primarily those from migrant, refugee and asylum seeking backgrounds

What we do

Practical language skills

The E3 Project

This free course aims to reduce isolation through English language skills, and is run pan-London.

Enabling higher education

The Research for Action and Influence Project

Supports BAMER individuals to gain the skills and confidence to be able to research issues affecting their communities

Advice and guidance

IAG & Pro bono

Free legal advice surgery and advice & guidance services

Capacity building

The Enhance Project

Support for Refugee and Migrant Community Organisations (RMCOs) to develop their voice and make a positive impact on their community

Practical language skills

ESOL Evening Classes

Free evening class for all levels (no entry requirements). Anyone is welcome!

Our history

The Evelyn Oldfield Unit is an independent, membership-based, charitable organization. Explore our history in a timeline of events below.

1992

Evelyn Oldfield was one of the first field officers for the City Parochial Foundation and the Trust for London, who died in 1992. Evelyn had devoted her energies to developing a strong and effective voluntary sector in which refugee organisations played a key part. The Evelyn Oldfield Unit is a testament to her vision and commitment.

1994

Our Founding

The Unit was established in 1994, by a consortium of funding bodies and agencies which work with refugee community organisations, including City Parochial Foundation, Thames Telethon, London Borough Grants, the Refugee Working Party and the Refugee Council.

Where to find us

We're located at

Evelyn Oldfield Unit
Resource for London
356 Holloway Road
London
N7 6PA

We’re open weekdays, 9am to 5pm

Once you are inside, ask the person at the reception to call us and we will come downstairs to welcome you.

By tube

The nearest station is Holloway Road station on the Piccadilly Line.

After you have exited the station, take a left and walk under the railway bridge for 5 minutes.

Our office is in the Resource for London building, on the opposite side of the road and can be reached by using the pedestrian crossing. We are next to the big Waitrose supermarket.

By bus

Buses 29, 43, 91, 153, 253, 259, 263, 271 and 393 stop within a short walk of Resource for London.

There are also mobility buses available in the area; these are numbers 920, 921, 923 and 927
